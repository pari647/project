Mahatma gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi, born Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi in 1869, was a pivotal figure in India's struggle for independence and a leader known for his unwavering commitment to non-violence and truth. He is revered as the "Father of the Nation" in India. Gandhi's philosophy of Satyagraha, which emphasized peaceful resistance and non-cooperation, inspired millions and played a crucial role in India's freedom movement. He also advocated for social justice, equality, and self-reliance, leaving a lasting legacy of peace and non-violent activism.
